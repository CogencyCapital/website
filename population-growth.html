Topic #2 - Population Growth

Cogency Capital

What & Why

The population is estimated to grow for the next 100 years (up to 10.4B, as projected by the UN), before beginning to decline. We want to look at different population growth projections, estimate its effects, and understand differences in growth between regions.

Questions

  • What regions will have the fastest population growth?
  • What regions will have the fastest population decline?
  • What are the implications of population growth?
  • Can world resources sustain a doubling in population?
  • What are the possibilities of black swan events affecting population growth?
  • What are the population growth differences between races?
  • How is belief and religion linked to population growth?
  • How is the wealth of a nation linked to population growth?
  • What would the world look like in terms of population in 50 and 100 years?

Findings

  • Education is the most significant factor for the decrease in fertility rates
  • Lower child mortality, access to birth control & family planning are highly correlated with lower growth in population
  • The world (by resources) will have no problem sustaining 10B people 

Predictions

  • Rapidly declining fertility rate due to education & access to birth control & abortion
  • A young population of a nation indicates long-term economic health
  • Africa & Asia will become the strongest economies simply due to their large young population (8 of 10 people will live in Africa or Asia by 2100)
  • Social media will drastically affect how people perceive having family
  • Europe will become the oldest region by age, with unclear viability around financing all the elderly people
  • Stronger campaigns to prevent population growth from getting too high (education, family planning, vaccines, social media indoctrination)
  • China has reached its peak population, and will decline from here onwards, resulting in IN surpassing CN in terms of economy
  • There will be a new voluntary growth spurt in highly educated and developed regions
  • A black swan event (biological/chemical attack, nuclear war) can not be excluded and might drastically alter projections
  • Massive opportunity growth in Africa & South Asia, massive opportunity/economic decline in Europe
