Topic #1 - Cryptocurrencies

Cogency Capital

What & Why

Cryptocurrencies have been trending in 2017 and in 2021 due to their opportunity to revolutionize traditional finance. Most notably, Bitcoin grew in popularity, gaining over 10000% to up to $69,000 per coin, raising the question of whether it can successfully lead the future of finance and serve as a digital alternative to Gold.

Questions

  • Could crypto replace traditional finance?
  • Can Bitcoin survive in the long term?
  • Can Bitcoin grow to more than 10% of Gold’s market capitalization?
  • Can crypto survive regulatory pressure?
  • What would the world look like with everyone using crypto?
  • What drove the previous bull runs? What might cause the next ones?
  • Does Bitcoin serve as a good inflation hedge?
  • What is the risk of black swan events?

Risks

  • Satoshi selling their stake of 1 million Bitcoins
  • Bitcoin being exposed as US government experiment, making RU/CN more suspicious of it
  • Extremely harsh regulations (consumer protection, environment, securities laws)
  • Competition with government-issued digital currencies
  • Tighter controls around Internet access
  • Quantum computing cracking SHA256
  • Network attacks and mining centralization
  • Anti-Proof of Work regulations
  • Harsh securities laws applied to most crypto tokens
  • Statistically likely that underlying tech fails or becomes exploited
  • Not enough demand for “digital gold”
  • Not enough performance to compete with centralized payment systems (CBDC)
  • No narratives to drive subsequent bull runs

Optimistic Predictions

  • Crypto & DeFi operate alongside central bank currencies successfully
  • CBDCs handle the payment layer, while DeFi/crypto handles all layer-2 applications
  • Bitcoin is successfully maintained, despite all security risks, enabling it to survive for the foreseeable future
  • Bitcoin gains acceptance as digital Gold and adoption grows for individuals, companies, governments
  • Bitcoin is just at the beginning of an S-curve adoption, with early adopters using the technology exclusively so far (we will see a massive adoption over the next 20-30 years)
  • Adoption will be driven by India, Asia, Latin America, and Africa, where most users just come online and have yet to discover the power of frictionless payments
  • Bitcoin will become easy-to-use, extremely fast with cheap fees through a layer-2 solution like the lightning network
  • Bitcoin will be denominated in sats, increasing the perception of owning “cheaper” Bitcoin
  • Crypto will build upon its strong values (trustlessness, anti-censorship, decentralization), out-competing CBDCs, which are trying to regulate, control, and censor their users

Pessimistic Predictions

  • Crypto struggles to gain adoption as central bank digital currencies push it out of the market
  • Crypto will become too regulated in regards to privacy, access (only professional investors), environmental laws, securities laws
  • A black-swan event (see risks) destroys all confidence in the early cryptocurrencies
  • End-users don’t care about decentralization
  • Original cryptos (Bitcoin etc.) persist in their use as storage of value while losing their use as a payment layer
  • Original cryptos (Bitcoin etc.) persist in their use as a payment layer while losing their use as storage of value
  • Bitcoin will not reach an S-curve adoption as expected (as compared to Internet or mobile phone adoption) due to a lack of use-case
