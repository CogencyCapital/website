Topic #6 - Belt and Road Initiative

Cogency Capital

What & Why

The Belt and Road Initiative is a massive infrastructure project spanning 147 countries based on the old Silk Road. The BRI will undoubtedly lead to an enormous increase in prosperity & growth for participating regions with China at its core. We are looking at the facts surrounding the BRI, the opportunities for the involved players and spectators, and the implications of this gigantic project.

Questions

  • Who are the beneficiaries of the Belt and Road Initiative?
  • Who are the opponents of the Belt and Road Initiative?
  • What are the risks and opportunities connected to BRI?
  • What are the arguments for/against the completion of BRI?
  • Is the BRI built with geopolitical/military goals in mind?
  • What is the expected growth of the participating regions after BRI completion?
  • Will the world order shift eastward caused by new BRI infrastructure?

Findings

  • BRI is a massive trade network based on the old silk road, official ongoing in development until 2049
  • BRI encompasses 147 countries from Sub-Saharan Africa, Europe/Central Asia, East Asia & Pacific, Latin America & Caribbean, Middle East & North Africa, South East Asia
  • BRI connects participating countries with roads & ports, which will lead to massive growth in prosperity
  • BRI promotes the flow of goods, investments, and talent, not just from/to China but within the participating countries as well
  • China is lending to other countries and helping them build the infrastructure (for the long term benefit of CN)
  • BRI will be highly beneficial for solving poverty in MENA regions and boosting global trade US/EU are worried about their power loss (announcing Build Back Better World as a response)
  • CN has the ability to execute, while EU/US democracies are too bureaucratic to get anything done
  • CN gives out loans to countries for infrastructure projects (which, if defaulted on, give CN the ability to control and seize the assets as collateral - cases of this already occurred in Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Djibouti, Laos, Tajikistan)
  • Poorer countries compromise on their sovereignty to take part in this project (which undoubtedly enriches China primarily, but the participants as well)
  • Africa is rich in natural resources and the fastest-growing region in the world, and China is taking full control of it by actually building/helping there
  • BRI ports are designed for strategic military use, hence BRI is clearly also built with military strategy in mind
  • 7 out of 10 of the world's largest construction companies are Chinese! CN is continuing to build while the rest of the world (mostly the West) is in stagnation
  • CN is willing to work with corrupt governments and is not restrained by moral limitations as US/Europe are (in regards to corruption, but also in regards to the environment, human rights, etc.)
  • B3W/PGII is a competing initiative to BRI, with the aim to reduce Chinese influence and promote G7 influence

Predictions

  • BRI regions will see immense growth in trade, investments, and prosperity
  • BRI will enable landlocked & isolated regions (for example, Stan regions) to prosper in global trade participation
  • A new world order will emerge around 2025-2035, with power shifting eastwards (Europe/US will lose power)
  • The globalists B3W/PGII will not be able to compete with BRI due to the inability to execute and internal problems (while BRI is already in progress, B3W/PGII is just an idea)
  • EU/US have 4-year time horizons and are mostly just looking at the short term, while CN has generational time horizons that can sustain such massive projects like the BRI
  • BRI will encounter many push-backs from individual countries financed by the US, with potential economic crises, regional wars, and civil unrest (but BRI will unlikely crumble as the globalists desire)
  • CN will replace the US as the main trade partner with BRI countries by 2030 (US is also doing its part, with its recession & induced collapse in demand for CN goods to counter inflation)
  • With China's complete control around ports, roads and capital, China is in a dominant position considering future negotiations - this will allow China to increase its leverage and influence
  • B3W/PGII won’t succeed since the West is constrained by self-imposed moral limitations (environment & sanctions), and the West has not been able to demonstrate capabilities to build cheap & efficient infrastructure that competes with China (the US does not even have high-speed railways)
