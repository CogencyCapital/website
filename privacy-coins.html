Topic #5 - Privacy Coins

Cogency Capital

What & Why

Privacy coins are cryptocurrencies that preserve anonymity by obscuring the flow of money across their networks. With the recent crack-down on Tornado Cash, and imposed blacklists on stablecoins (Circle’s USDC), privacy coins are becoming more and more relevant. We want to explore popular privacy coins and estimate opportunities & market size for privacy-based payments.

Questions

  • What is the current market cap of privacy coins?
  • Is it possible that privacy coins will grow in demand more than crypto in general?
  • How will CBDCs and government regulation on KYC affect privacy coins?
  • What are the most promising privacy coins?
  • How many transactions on-chain are private?

Findings

  • Monero, Zcash, Decred, Oasis Network, Horizen, Secret, Keep Network, Phala Network, Verge, Dusk Network, PIVX, Navcoin are the most popular privacy coins
  • Ring signatures, stealth addresses, identity masking, zero-knowledge proofs, tx-amount obfuscation are the most common technologies used by privacy coins
  • Privacy coins are used less for illegal activity as assumed, and more to protect personal privacy for legal payments, cross-border payments, donations, etc.
  • Only 0.15% of all crypto transactions are related to illegal activity
  • Privacy coin transactions make up only 1-2% of all crypto transactions

Risks

  • Regulation will be too tight on privacy coins, making them effectively useless (with its only use case being criminal activity)
  • Privacy might not be valued enough by most users, especially when compared to transaction speed or access to handouts/benefits as would be the case with CBDCs

Predictions

  • Privacy coins are unlikely to work with smart contracts due to their anonymous nature, and as such, will likely focus on payments exclusively
  • Privacy coins are already banned in KR, AU, JP, and more countries will likely ban them at the risk of illegal activity
  • If regulations crack down on payment privacy, a parallel “gray” economy might emerge (and not for illegal activity, but rather just to preserve privacy)
  • Implementation of CBDCs will cause a discussion around privacy, with the main argument in favor being the “greater good” and “public security” - this might become a trigger for a subset of users deciding to switch to privacy-protecting coins
  • As with most technologies, early adopters are using the tech for shady activity, but with time mainstream users will adopt privacy tokens for day-to-day transfers

Opportunities

  • Betting on the price increase of privacy coins, relative to Bitcoin (especially as CBDC implementations are getting closer)
  • Currently, privacy coins have a bad reputation of being used mostly for illegal activity, which might change rapidly once regular users start using them more and more
