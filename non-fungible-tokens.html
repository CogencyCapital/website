Topic #4 - Non Fungible Tokens

Cogency Capital

What & Why

Non-fungible tokens are digital asset representations, most commonly used for digital art (images, videos). NFTs have seen an immense increase in demand during the 2021/2022 bull run. We are investigating whether this trend was short-lived, whether NFTs are here to stay long-term, and what NFT use cases could look like in the future.

Questions

  • What are the most common NFT use cases right now?
  • What could be the most common NFT use cases in the future?
  • Were NFTs just a short-lived fad, or is there more behind them?
  • What is the potential market for digital art?
  • What is the current market of physical art?
  • What are optimistic/pessimistic cases for NFT adoption?

Findings

  • Depending on NFT regulation (whether NFTs are classified as security or utility) the market might see more/less growth
  • The global art market value is approx. 65 billion USD per year, and it is reasonable to assume the digital art market could capture 10-20% of that (similar to how Bitcoin grew to approx. 10% of Golds market value)
  • Most NFT projects come and go quickly, with only 2 out of the top 10 projects being more than a year old (Cryptopunks and Bored Apes)
  • Liquidity seems to be the most significant problem for NFTs, with demand having dried up rapidly after the initial hype
  • NFTs with utility are becoming more popular, such as NFTs granting unique benefits to holders
  • Only approx. 10% are buying NFTs for art (long-term), 90% are buying to make a profit or to flex to the community (short-term)

Predictions

  • Fractional NFTs will dominate in the future due to better liquidity and easier access to own a piece of a whole
  • Physical assets will see more digitization through NFTs, especially with fractional NFTs enabling users to hold parts of physical assets such as land and real estate
  • NFTs use cases for music, ticketing, gift cards, access passes, game items/skins will increase, while the use-case for digital art will likely grow at a slower pace
  • NFTs will have to present unique utility value, brand value (such as Nike Sneaker NFTs) or physical value (fractional real estate, etc.) to gain demand
  • The days of unknown artists releasing a collection of 10000 similar looking collectibles purely on hype are over
  • Demand for NFT collections will be highly skewed towards top projects, even more as with fungible tokens, with top 0.1-1% receiving 90-99% of all trading activity (primarily due to the lack of liquidity)
  • Gaming will offer the best use-case for NFTs, with tradable in-game items, skins and avatars on the blockchain
  • The high-end/luxury market will see an increase in NFT demand, especially when combined with brand value and perceived uniqueness
  • Bitcoin could become the most dominant way to issue NFTs (via the Taro protocol on the Lightning Network)
  • While many 2017 alt-tokens saw their demand increase once again during the 2021 bull market, most NFT projects released in 2021/2022 will likely die (due to lack of fractional liquidity)
  • It is unlikely that NFTs will receive as much hype as they did in 2021 ever again - we expect that NFT demand will slowly recover to all-time-highs, although over many years and with drastically different use-cases (mostly powered by gaming and virtual economy)
  • NFTs will be based on the actual value they represent (such as ticket value, digital fractions of physical assets, in-game item/skin, access/governance passes), as opposed to speculative art value except in a few rare cases
