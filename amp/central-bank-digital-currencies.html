Topic #3 - Central Bank Digital Currencies

By Cogency Capital October 17, 2022

What & Why

Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs) have been in discussion recently by the Federal Reserve and ECB, with China, Russia, and India having already launched some form of CBDC.

CBDCs are digital payment systems, with users owning accounts directly with the central bank (instead of intermediary banks). Central banks have exclusive control over the digital currency, which raises questions about potential abuse of power and dangers such as Orwellian control, surveillance, and censorship.

Questions

  • What are the advantages/disadvantages of CBDCs compared to Bitcoin?
  • What are the advantages/disadvantages of CBDCs compared to the current way banks work?
  • How dangerous are CBDCs regarding freedom, free speech & censorship?
  • How likely will central banks introduce negative interest rates to promote spending?
  • How likely will central banks start to promote social credit type of schemes?
  • Is there a danger in central banks using the collected payment data against users?
  • Can inflation control and GDP growth be controlled better with CBDCs?
  • What is the tradeoff between the efficiency of digital money vs. censorship and control?

Optimistic Predictions

  • CBDCs will offer better safety for the majority of the population due to the simple way to track and intervene
  • Since CBDCs are provided by a single institution, it will be easy to interlink CBDCs with each other, offering efficient and cheap cross-border payment solutions
  • People will not see any difference between credit cards and CBDCs (possibly even benefits in payment speed and cost)
  • People will likely receive “free money” to switch from existing systems (the switch will happen at the perceived free will of people)
  • CBDCs are the dream for socialist-driven politics, and naturally, a large part of society will openly and enthusiastically welcome CBDCs with their potential benefits for the “greater good”
  • CBDCs provide an excellent narrative for owning gold and crypto (and will likely trigger the largest bull run for these hard assets that provide privacy and ownership control)
  • Prevents counterfeiting, avoidance of crime/tax evasion, more financial inclusion
  • Direct cash injections benefitting (in the short-term) individual races, groups, or well-behaving people according to policies that central authorities want to encourage
  • Negative interest rates to encourage spending and increase the velocity of money
  • Programmable money, open APIs for 3rd party developers, easier (although automatic) taxation through automatic deductions from accounts
  • Tax authorities can drastically reduce their staff and operational expenses since taxes can be easily calculated and deducted automatically (same applies for credited tax refunds)
  • A massive demand for privacy/payment-oriented cryptocurrencies that are truly trustless & decentralized (people will vote with their behavior and eventually replace centralized CBDCs due to declining purchasing power and impractical policies/regulations)
  • The free market/open source developers will be more agile and reactive to real-time demands by users (e-CNY is inferior to WeChat pay and Alipay), causing users to prefer cryptos over CBDCs out of convenience
  • Getting users to change from credit cards/cryptos to CBDCs (including installing apps, identification software, etc.) will require immense upfront incentives such as money handouts (or an orchestrated panic, either via a cyber attack on financial systems or massive market crash)
  • It is unlikely CBDCs will gain broad acceptance due to inconvenience unless the public is forced to use them (it will be a battle between forced agenda and human nature)

Pessimistic Predictions

  • A social credit system is the logical next step after having complete control over people’s spending data
  • CO2/energy credits and similar consumption credits can be easily enforced and likely will be enforced
  • A total ban on cash and cryptos, making CBDC use mandatory through regulation (if people are given the option to decide, CBDCs will unlikely gain acceptance as seen with eCNY)
  • All intermediary banks will go bankrupt through an orchestrated financial crash, and unlike in 2008 these banks won’t be rescued
  • CBDCs encourage central planning, with governments likely overstepping into socialist/full control measures (it is almost impossible for free markets to flourish with authoritarian control by one party over the payment layer)
  • With the obvious incompetence by central banks, it is almost guaranteed that CBDCs will turn out horribly for people (with all the expected dangers like censorship, frozen accounts, and social credit systems)
  • CBDCs will cause sanctions against individuals, companies, and nations, who don’t comply with the policies of central authorities
  • Control over payments, connected to the rise of artificial intelligence (facial recognition, etc.) poses a threat to freedom, with some form of social credit system being only a matter of time
  • The balance sheets of central banks are technically infinite and don’t need to be balanced with assets; hence central banks can create money even easier than before and hand it out based on policies/narratives instead of real economic value
  • Progressive taxation based on usage (gas/energy, meat consumption, etc.)
  • Gives the central bank complete control over means of production, using artificial intelligence to evaluate the collected payment data (a socialists dream) - USSR top-down centrally planned market failed because decisions were based on delayed estimates & incomplete information - CBDC real-time evaluation of payment data could cause a centrally planned economy that actually appears to work
  • No competition between banks to offer better terms, service, interest rates, etc.
  • Automatic deductions of penalties from accounts, whether in the form of one-time deduction (speeding ticket) or varying debit based on some formula (CO2 credits, etc.)
  • CBDCs will be introduced to the market by providing extensive handouts and significant incentives (to get people to change the status quo)
  • Long-term effects of CBDC handouts will likely cause massive inflation and inefficiencies (that interventionists will try to resolve through more policies & regulations)
  • Transition to CBDCs will be met with a lot of resistance (especially in the West), but will eventually be forced upon people through strictly outlawing cash (rhetoric will change as it did with the requirement of vaccines, from voluntary and recommended to mandatory for the greater good fighting yet another invisible enemy)
  • The next logical step after CBDCs (or parallel to it) is the introduction of an identification system for users (accessible as API for 3rd parties) - this comes with additional dangers in regards to privacy, access control, and censorship
  • CBDCs will likely become transitory currencies that people will try to exchange for hard assets (like in AR/CU/VE)
  • As long as the government & central banks are honest, CBDCs seem like an advantage, however, if governments start abusing the additional power for their own interest, the downsides massively outweigh the upsides of a CBDC
  • Stimulus checks/UBI will become the norm for income while the workforce is slowly replaced by AI & redundant labor
  • CBDCs are more restrictive and provide less utility than cryptos, making them the unpopular choice compared to cryptos and other digital payment methods
  • The central bank will be the sole trusted authority and prone to internal/external cyber attacks and other “single points of failure” that could be largely avoided in a decentralized/trustless system
  • Central banks are intentionally discrediting the innovation around cryptocurrencies (decentralization, openness, privacy) to maintain control and power
  • Cryptocurrencies (especially payment & privacy-oriented ones) will be regulated out of existence (or made inaccessible by most people), since they compete directly with CBDCs

Opportunities

  • Long payment cryptocurrencies for crypto adoption (Bitcoin with its lightning network)
  • Long privacy cryptocurrencies for privacy narrative (Monero, Zcash, Verge, Navcoin)
  • Short banks/payment companies for replacement narrative (JPM, BAC, WFC, PYPL)
  • Exit the traditional banking system (gold, self-sufficiency, geographical diversification)
